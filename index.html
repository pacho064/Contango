Methodology & data sources

Contango is built on a core argument: the benchmark prices that dominate financial media — ICE Brent, CME WTI — do not reflect what producers in the Middle East are actually receiving, or what buyers are actually paying. The gap between these benchmarks and local Official Selling Prices (OSPs) is the signal this platform is built to surface.

What is an OSP?

An Official Selling Price is the price at which a national oil company — Aramco, ADNOC, SOMO, ADNOC — actually sells crude to its term customers for a given month's loading. These are set monthly, typically as a differential to a benchmark (e.g. Oman/Dubai average, Brent). When Oman crude trades at $161 while Brent sits at $138, that $23 premium is not noise — it is signal. It reflects sour crude scarcity, Asian refinery demand, and the geopolitical risk premium that Brent does not price in.

The Yanbu argument

Saudi Arabia's East-West pipeline (Petroline) carries up to 5 million bbl/day from Abqaiq in the Eastern Province to Yanbu on the Red Sea. It is frequently cited as the strategic bypass of the Strait of Hormuz. What this argument omits: Yanbu's export terminal capacity is approximately 1.8 million bbl/day. The pipeline is not the bottleneck. The port is. Houthi operations at Bab el-Mandeb then add a second chokepoint to this already constrained route, making the bypass argument structurally weak at current port capacity.

Data sources

DME — Dubai Mercantile Exchange
Oman crude futures and spot assessments. The most liquid local benchmark for Middle East sour crude.
ADNOC OSP
Abu Dhabi National Oil Company monthly Official Selling Prices for Murban, Upper Zakum, Das, and Umm Lulu grades.
SOMO Iraq
State Organisation for Marketing of Oil — monthly OSPs for Basra Light and Basra Heavy.
EIA Weekly Petroleum Status
US Energy Information Administration. Cushing stocks, US crude production, and refinery utilisation.
Reuters Energy
Breaking news feed for commodity markets, tanker movements, and geopolitical events affecting supply.
MEES — Middle East Economic Survey
The authoritative subscription publication for Middle East oil and gas. Used for cross-referencing OSP data.
OPEC Secretariat
Monthly Oil Market Report and JMMC statements for quota and compliance data.
World Port Index (NGA)
US National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency. Port coordinates, depth, and capacity data for all terminals.
