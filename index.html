Infrastructure spotlight

The Yanbu bottleneck

Saudi Arabia's East-West Pipeline (Petroline) is routinely cited as the solution to Strait of Hormuz vulnerability. The logic: if Hormuz is disrupted, redirect exports through the pipeline to Yanbu on the Red Sea.

This argument has a structural flaw: the pipeline can carry 5 million barrels per day. Yanbu's export terminal capacity is approximately 1.8 million barrels per day.

The pipeline is not the bottleneck. The port is.
Even fully utilising the East-West Pipeline would allow Saudi Arabia to bypass Hormuz for ~18% of its daily exports. The remaining ~8.4M bbl/d remain Hormuz-dependent.

Then add Bab el-Mandeb. Saudi crude routed via Yanbu must still transit this strait — currently the highest-risk chokepoint in the world, with Houthi operations ongoing. The "bypass" trades Hormuz exposure for Bab el-Mandeb exposure, at lower capacity.

How every figure is built

What is an OSP?

Official Selling Prices and what they reveal

An Official Selling Price is the price at which a national oil company — Saudi Aramco, ADNOC, SOMO Iraq, QatarEnergy — sells crude to its term customers for a given loading month. These are set monthly, typically as a differential to a benchmark such as the Oman/Dubai average or Brent Dated.

When Oman crude settles at $161 while ICE Brent trades at $138, that $23 premium is not a rounding error. It reflects sour crude scarcity in Asian markets, the risk premium on Middle East supply routes, and demand signals that a London-traded futures contract does not capture.

Risk classification

How risk tiers are assigned

Risk tiers on this platform are qualitative, not numeric. They are derived from three sources: US State Department Travel Advisory levels (1–4), ACLED armed conflict event data, and Lloyd's Market Association Joint War Committee area designations for maritime risk.

Tiers are Low / Moderate / Elevated / High. Every risk label shows its source and last updated date. We do not generate proprietary risk scores.

Data policy

Credibility over completeness

Prices marked "indicative" are compiled from publicly available NOC disclosures and market assessments. Where real-time data is unavailable, we show the most recent verified figure with a clear update date. We do not display real-time trading data. This platform is not financial advice.
